Combined cycle

The combined cycle is a combined of the turbogenerator energy converter with RANKINE cycle (bottoming cycle). Based on the IRReGT under development technology and the ORC experience, we are working on a hight efficiency combined cycle for solar concentrated application(Hélios)

RANKINE CYCLE

Known also as vapor cycle, Rankine machines
uses changing phase working fluid as
working medium to achieve the Rankine
thermodynamic cycle. This cycle uses a pump,
a boiler (evaporator), an expander and a condenser.
Water is used fot medium and high temperature
sources and organic fluids for low temperature.
We have designed different Rankine systems
for low, medium and high temperature. Different
expansion machines have been developed including:
piston expander, turbocompressors,
scroll and impulse turbines.

SPLIT CYCLE ENGINE

Split Cycle Engines are high efficiency
Internal Combustion Engine where intake
and compression phases are separated
from the combustion/expansion and exhaust
phases.
This energy converters offers different
advantages compared to conventional ICE:
-Thermal management can offer higher
performance and efficiency
-Miller cycle can be achieved by simply
increasing the length of the power cylinder.
-Piston friction can be reduced by offsetting
the compression and power cylinders
-Design flexibility of the engine
-Compatibility with additional devices
like turbocharger and waste heat recovery
-Higher thermal efficiencies can be achieved
by using an internal regenerator
We design an develop these Engines with
power ranging from 1kW to 100 kW
We design all components and we define
the integration validation plan during
the development phase and we perform
characterization and endurance tests

STIRLING

Stirling engines are external
combustion engines that can be
used as APU (Auxiliary Power Unit)
and as WHR (waste heat recovery) machines
Stirling engines are reversible
machines can be used also as heat
pumps while converting electric
power to heat (hot and cold)
Stirling machines offer the
following advantages:
Good efficiency
No nocif working fluid
Low material cost
No lubrication required and no
components’ wear
Tri-generation (electricity, hot and cold)b
and multi-fuel capability in case of
cogeneration machine
We design and develop Stirling prototypes
for different applications, with power
ranging from 2 kWe to 20 kWe. We developed
a recuperative reheat Stirling machine
(patent architecture) with high efficiency
and power density and reduced emissions.
The machine is fully capsulated and the
electric generator is driven through
a magnetic clutch.

ThermoElectric Generator

Thermoelectric generators convert the
temperature difference directly into
electric energy. These machines are
know for their silent operation.
They require no moving components and
have no environmental side effects.
They uses specific materials to convert
the heat power into electric power.
We have developed and tested
high merit coefficient thermoelectric
generators and patented some
architecture where TEG can be
used as Auxiliary Power Unit.
